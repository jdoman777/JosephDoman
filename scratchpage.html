Harpy Eagles

General Stats | Habitat | Interesting Facts | Harpy Eagle Video | Deforestation Graph | Harpy Eagle Game

  1. General Stats
    • As one of the largest species of eagle, Harpy eagles grow from 36-40 inches. Females weigh 13-20 lbs, with males weighing 9-11 lbs.
    • They have a wingspan of 6 and a half feet, much higher than the global average of human height.
    • Harpy eagles have a lifespan of 35-45 years.
    • Harpy eagles are monogamous (they mate for life) and typically stay with their partner for 25-30 years.
    • They do not migrate, typically establishing a permanent territory with tall trees and plentiful prey (they're homebodies I guess).
  2. Habitat
    • They typically live in Central and South America.
    • They inhabit the rain forests and prefer to perch closer to the ground than a lot of birds.
    • They roost in massive nests (the size of double beds!) around 130 feet from the ground.
    • Ecosystems that have this bird are more likely to be healthy because they require balanced ecosystems to survive.
  3. Interesting Facts
    • The Harpy eagle is actually the inspiration for Fawkes the Phoenix from Harry Potter.
    • The Harpy eagle is the national bird of Panama.
    • The feathers on a harpy eagle's face ("disk feathers") can be moved to funnel sound like an owl's, which drastically improves their hearing
    • Their hearing complements their insane eyesight, which is around 8 times better than a human's.
    • They have talons the same size as a grizzly bear's claw, with a grip strength of around 530 psi. This could easily crush a human skull.
